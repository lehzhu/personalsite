## Scaffold for Completion

### 1. Open with the letter
- Name "Letter from Birmingham Jail" directly.
- Start with the line, idea, or tension from it that still feels alive to you.

### 2. What the letter is actually about
- Not just civil rights history, but the ethics of urgency, moderation, and moral clarity.
- Explain why King's audience matters as much as his enemies.

### 3. Why it matters now
- Draw the bridge to a current social or institutional pattern you care about.
- Focus on delayed justice, polite obstruction, or the preference for order over truth.

### 4. Your personal angle
- What in the letter challenges your own instincts?
- Where do you see yourself: the impatient actor, the observer, the moderate, the organizer?

### 5. Broader argument
- What does the letter teach about how change actually happens?
- Why respectable institutions often fail moral tests precisely when they appear calm and reasonable.

### 6. Ending
- Close with one principle from the letter that you want to carry into your own life or work.
