## Scaffold for Completion

### 1. Finish the core definition
- Consistency as predictability.
- Consistency as principled decision-making.
- Inconsistency as either adaptability or incoherence.

### 2. Separate kinds of institutions
- Corporations.
- Universities.
- Governments.
- Clubs or communities.

### 3. Explain why institutional consistency feels different
- Larger blast radius.
- Slower feedback loops.
- Human incentives get absorbed into process and norms.

### 4. Case studies
- One institution that is consistently excellent because it can update.
- One that is "consistent" only in preserving its own inertia.

### 5. Tension to resolve
- When should institutions stay legible and stable?
- When should they become radically inconsistent in order to survive?

### 6. Ending
- Offer a test for whether an institution's consistency is a virtue or a warning sign.
